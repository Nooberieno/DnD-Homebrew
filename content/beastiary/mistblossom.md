_**Rooted Body.**_  The Mistblossom is fixed in place unless magically transplanted or uprooted by specific environmental effects. It cannot be knocked prone and is immune to forced movement.

_**Neutralizing Gas.**_  Creatures within 30 feet of the monster can’t benefit from features, traits, feats, or other effects that are not spells, magic items, or class abilities. This includes traits such as Magic Resistance, Regeneration, Blindsight, Devil’s Sight, Pack Tactics, and similar abilities. Spellcasting functions normally. 
The gas cannot suppress any abilities that purely rely on a creatures physical body, such as natural armor, movement modes, claws, limbs, size and other structural anatomy. 

 An easy intuitive way to find out what you would lose is to check If an ability would cease to function if the creature were turned into a mundane animal or human of the same size and type, if it would cease to function, Neutralizing Gas suppresses it.


### Actions


_**Vine Lash.**_  Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 20 ft., one target. Hit: 10 (2d6+3) bludgeoning damage, and the target must succeed on a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be pulled up to 10 feet toward the Mistblossom.

_**Sleep Spores (Recharge 5–6).**_  The Mistblossom releases a cloud of golden dust in a 30-foot radius. Each creature in that area must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or fall unconscious for 1 minute. A creature wakes if it takes damage or if another creature uses an action to shake it awake.


### Reactions


_**Thrashing Vines.**_  When a creature the Mistblossom can sense moves within 10 feet of it, the plant makes a Vine Lash attack against that creature.
